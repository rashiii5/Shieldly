🔍 Suspicious URL Awareness

In today’s digital world, **one bad click** can open the door to identity theft, account compromise, or malware infection. Cybercriminals often disguise malicious URLs to trick users into visiting fake websites that steal your data.

⚠️ What Can Go Wrong If You Click a Suspicious Link?

  • 💳 Phishing attacks that steal your login credentials and financial info.
  • 🦠 Malware downloads that infect your device without you realizing.
  • 📸 Spyware or keyloggers that monitor what you type or access.
  • 🔐 Account takeovers via fake login pages or password reset scams.

📩 Where Do Suspicious Links Appear?

  • Emails that urge you to verify, update, or reset passwords
  • SMS messages pretending to be from banks, delivery companies, or government bodies
  • Social media messages offering free giveaways or “shocking” content
  • Fake websites in search results or online ads

📚 How to Read a URL

Understanding how a URL is structured helps detect fake or malicious links.

https:// www. example.com /login ?user=abc
  • Protocol: https:// – Always look for HTTPS for secure communication.
  • Subdomain: www. – Or other prefixes like mail., account.
  • Domain: example.com – This is the real site identity. Be alert for lookalikes like go0gle.com or secure-example-login.com.
  • Path: /login – The specific page you're accessing.
  • Query: ?user=abc – Sometimes contains data; be careful what you enter in such URLs.

🚩 Red Flags in Suspicious URLs

  • Weird TLDs like .ru, .xyz, .top, etc.
  • Typosquatting: e.g., go0gle.com instead of google.com
  • Presence of @ symbols (used to trick users)
  • Too many numbers in the domain (e.g., free123secure-login.ru)
  • Double-encoded text or unreadable paths

🔍 How Can You Spot a Suspicious URL?

  • Weird-looking domain names (like go0gle.com or secure-paypa1.com)
  • Too many hyphens, numbers, or encoded characters
  • Use of shady top-level domains like .xyz, .ru, .top, etc.
  • Presence of @ symbols, which can redirect to hidden destinations

🛠️ What Can You Do to Stay Safe?

  • Always hover over a link to preview the real URL
  • Don’t click links in emails or texts from unknown senders
  • Use a **link checker tool** like the one in Shieldy before opening a suspicious URL
  • Keep your browser, antivirus, and operating system updated
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable 2FA on all accounts

💡 Even if a link looks safe, double-checking is never a bad idea.
